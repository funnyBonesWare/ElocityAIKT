Before AI vs. After AI — By Stage

Quality & Confidence Improvements

Evidence-Based Shipping
Before: "I tested it locally, it works" — no proof, no screenshots, trust-based QA.
After: Every MR has Playwright screenshots, pass/fail report, and AI review log attached. Verifiable evidence, not hope.
Bug Prevention
Before: Bugs discovered in production. Hotfix cycle: 4-8 hours. Customer impact: real.
After: AI review catches 80%+ of issues before merge. E2E tests catch the rest. -21% escaped defects.
Developer Experience
Before: Engineers spent 40%+ time on boilerplate, context-switching, and waiting for reviews.
After: +2.3 hrs/day of focus time recovered. Engineers work on hard problems, not repetitive tasks.

Team-Level Impact

Throughput Multiplier
Our engineering team effectively operates at 2-3x the output of a similarly-sized team without AI tools.
A 5-person team with AI delivers what a 12-15 person team delivers traditionally — same quality, better evidence trail.
This isn't about replacing engineers. It's about making every engineer dramatically more productive.
Sprint Velocity
Story points/sprint: Increased by ~40% after AI integration, with the same team size.
PR merge rate: More PRs merged per sprint with fewer revision cycles — AI catches issues on first pass.
Incident rate: Trending down. Fewer production incidents thanks to automated evidence and AI review gates.
Bottom Line
The ROI of AI in our workflow is clear: ~50% faster delivery, 85% faster reviews, 21% fewer bugs, and 2.3 hours of focus time recovered daily — per engineer. We're still improving — not perfect yet — but the trajectory is undeniable. The tools pay for themselves within the first few sprints.
