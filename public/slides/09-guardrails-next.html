Guardrails, Quality & What's Next for Elocity

AI is powerful — but it's not infallible. Here's why we still need humans in the loop, the rules we enforce on AI output, how we ensure quality, and our ambitious roadmap ahead.

Why We Still Need Humans

AI Hallucinates
AI can generate confident-sounding code that is subtly wrong — incorrect business logic, made-up API endpoints, or assumptions that don't match reality. Humans catch what AI invents.
Context Is King
AI doesn't understand your business domain, customer pain points, or organizational context. Humans bridge the gap between technically correct and actually useful. Architecture decisions need human judgment.
Accountability
When something breaks in production, a human is responsible — not the AI. Human review ensures someone owns the decision to ship. AI assists, humans are accountable.

Our Guardrails — Rules We Enforce on AI

Code Quality Rules
Rule 1: All AI-generated code must pass linting + type checks before review.
Rule 2: No PR exceeds 500 lines — keeps changes reviewable and rollback-safe.
Rule 3: Every feature must have E2E test coverage via Playwright MCP.
Rule 4: AI review + human review required for merge — both must pass.
Rule 5: Prompt templates standardize how we instruct AI — consistent, auditable.
Security & Data Rules
Rule 1: No customer data in AI prompts — ever. Prompts use anonymized or mock data only.
Rule 2: AI cannot modify database schemas or run migrations without explicit human approval.
Rule 3: Environment secrets managed via vault — AI never sees raw credentials.
Rule 4: All AI interactions logged and auditable for compliance.
Rule 5: Third-party AI model outputs treated as untrusted input — always validated.

What's Next for Elocity

We're not done. We're actively building toward AI-native — where AI is the default, not the exception.

Our Vision: Becoming AI-Native
Elocity is on a mission to become a truly AI-native engineering team. This means AI isn't a bolt-on tool — it's embedded into every step of how we think, build, test, and ship. We're a lean team, and AI is how we punch above our weight. Every day, we're refining our AI workflows to produce more, faster, with higher quality — without growing headcount.
Target: 1-Day Feature Delivery
Current state: ~1 week average for feature completion. Good, but not where we want to be.
Target: Bring average feature delivery down to 1 day — from ticket to production-ready with evidence.
How: Better prompt engineering, more autonomous agents, smarter test generation, faster review cycles.
Improving Daily
Culture: Every sprint, we identify friction points in our AI workflow and fix them. Continuous improvement is the strategy.
Lean team advantage: Small team = fast decisions. We adopt new AI tools and techniques the same week they release.
Compounding: Each improvement builds on the last. The team that improves its AI workflow fastest wins.

Roadmap — What We're Building Next

Smarter Agents
Now: AI generates code from prompts.
Next: Agents that read the Jira ticket, plan the implementation, write code, run tests, and self-correct — with minimal human steering.
Auto Test Generation
Now: Playwright MCP runs tests we define.
Next: AI reads the feature, automatically generates comprehensive E2E tests, runs them, and reports evidence — zero manual test writing.
Requirements Intelligence
Now: Humans write acceptance criteria.
Next: AI converts messy requirements into structured specs — edge cases, assumptions, and clear definition of done — before a single line of code is written.
The Elocity Principle
AI is our multiplier, not our replacement. We're a lean team that operates at 2-3x our size because of AI — but every decision, every merge, every deployment has a human behind it. Our goal isn't to remove engineers from the loop. It's to make every engineer so productive that a small team can build what large teams struggle to deliver. We're not perfect yet — but we're getting better every single day.
